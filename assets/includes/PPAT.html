PPAT POEM Assessment Tool

1. Planning

1 - Very poor = Cannot justify reasons for choice, clear obstruction to tunnel in chosen orientation, unable to identify anterior or posterior position, even with prompting necessitating mentor take over

5 - Very Good = Justifiable, independent, corroborated choice of orientation, no obstruction to tunnel in chosen orientation, avoids tortuosity/diverticulae/fibrosis, leaves maximum choice open to patient in future. Correctly identifies orientation based on gastric landmarks and gravity

1 - Very poor = Unable to identify the GOJ despite prompting, unable to comment on the nature of the GOJ, does not consider pseudoachalasia as a cause

5 - Very Good = Accurately and independently identifies and documents the GOJ using multiple techniques. Appreciates puckering and any resistance to scope passage. Considers the possibility of pseudoachalasia

1 - Very poor = Unable to locate and justify the mucosotomy site, despite mentor input. Does not consider spastic segments, length of myotomy or orientation

5 - Very Good = Independent, justifiable identification of the mucosotomy site. Considers length of myotomy, length of spastic segment, appreciates the spastic segment endoscopically, appropriately allows enough space for the myotomy

1 - Very poor = Unable to identify or justify the site, the length and completeness of the myotomy despite prompting. Does not consider spastic segments, available literature and implications on post POEM GERD

5 - Very Good = Independent, justifiable identification of the myotomy site and length. Considers spastic segments, available evidence and understands the importance of the gastric length of the myotomy

1 - Very poor = Does not examine the esophagus prior to incision. Unable to adequately cleanse the esophagus, risking contamination of the tunnel. Does not appreciate risk factors for food stasis

5 - Very Good = Meticulously examines and cleans the esophagus prior to mucosotomy. Appropriate use of the water jet and mucolytics. Examines for early esophageal cancer. Able to identify factors which may increase the risk of food stasis

2. Mucosotomy

1 - Very poor = Unable to find the submucosal plane despite guidance. Poor technique, no communication with nursing staff to begin injection prior to advancing the needle, does not utilize the stab and pull back technique

5 - Very Good = Quickly, efficiently and independently finds the submucosal plane. Caution taken to avoid mucosal and intra-thoracic injection

1 - Very poor = Once the needle is in the submucosal space there is no movement of the needle during injection. Static injection not achieving an adequate lift

5 - Very Good = Once the needle is in the submucosal plane there is graduated dynamic movement away from the submucosa to achieve an adequate lift

1 - Very poor = Inadequate volume of injectate used resulting in a poor lift despite prompting. Lift compromises the safety of the mucosotomy by risking muscle injury. Unable to achieve adequate lift despite verbal prompts

5 - Very Good = Independently and purposely injects an adequate volume to obtain a lasting lift large enough to facilitate a safe mucosotomy incision

1 - Very poor = Uncontrolled, unsafe use of the knife/ESU. Does not withdraw the knife to check planes. Unable to identify planes and cutting line. Knife tip held tangentially to the mucosa

5 - Very Good = Controlled, independent, slow, stepwise, sequential single taps on the ESU. Confidently recognize planes, ensures incision passes the muscularis mucosa but maintains a safe margin from the muscularis propria. Knife tip held against the mucosa at 45 – 80 degrees

1 - Very poor = Uncontrolled, unsafe extension of the incision. Direction not maintained, incorrect plane, inappropriate length, inadequate lift. Unable to correct with verbal prompts requires mentor take over for safety

5 - Very Good = Precise, independent, controlled, stepwise, vertical extension of the incision maintaining the direction of the tunnel. Gentle traction applied to the caudal end. Appropriate length to facilitate entry. Repeated use of submucosal injection

3. Tunnel entry

1 - Very poor = Uncontrolled, non-purposeful movements of the knife, trims incorrect area, inadequate lift, risks thermal injury to the mucosa and/or muscle layer, trimming does not improve tunnel access. Necessitates mentor take over for safety despite prompts

5 - Very Good = Careful, precise, controlled, independently trims the submucosa at the caudal incision end. Utilizes the cap to protect the mucosa from thermal injury. Uses injection to improve safety

1 - Very poor = Unable to enter the tunnel despite guidance. Inappropriate use of the tunneling cap, excessive force applied, does not recognize inadequately opened tunnel

5 - Very Good = Independent, safe, gentle manipulation of the tunnelling cap to burrow under the incision, uses the cap to enhance the mucosa/muscle plane

1 - Very poor = Fails to recognize incision length as a limitation to tunnel entry. Excessive force applied risking tearing of the mucosotomy

5 - Very Good = Easily, safely and independently able to enter the tunnel. Ensures adequate incision length to facilitate completion and closure. Early recognition of too short an incision and extends incision appropriately

4. Submucosal tunneling

1 - Very poor = Infrequent or non-use of submucosal injection compromising safety. Rapid instrument changes overshooting the catheter beyond the scope, unsafe use of an injector needle.

5 - Very Good = Repeated use of injection at submucosal muscle interface to maintain a safe plane. Where a jet knife is not available, uses blunt injection preferentially. Safely exchanges instruments and avoids mucosal injury when extending the needle

1 - Very poor = Poor appreciation of the cutting plane, dissects close to the mucosa, creates traction on tissue with the knife resulting in slippage of the knife, applies thermal energy close to the mucosa. Unsafe dissection despite guidance from the mentor

5 - Very Good = Precise, controlled dissection along the muscle using rotation of the right hand, continuously ensures the mucosal layer is kept away from the dissection plane. Uses spray coagulation without contact and traction on tissue.

1 - Very poor = Does not periodically check direction, continues to dissect unaware of deviations in direction, unable to determine the direction of the GOJ

5 - Very Good = Periodically checks the direction of the tunnel by withdrawing the scope. Able to timeously identify any deviations in direction and correct it.

1 - Very poor = Repeated, uncontrolled, uneven, blind dissection resulting in the creation of multiple tunnels, displays poor appreciation of planes despite prompting. Failure to recognize creating a dissection plane in the muscle. Uneven width of the tunnel with too narrow or large areas

5 - Very Good = Meticulous, purposeful, controlled, stepwise dissection from one end of the tunnel to the other. Avoids creating multiple planes. Cognizant of not creating a dissection plane in the muscle layer. Maintains width of the tunnel

1 - Very poor = Continued dissection failing to perform safety checks, unable to anticipate challenges, failure to recognize adverse events

5 - Very Good = Periodically checks tunnel direction, mucosal integrity, proximity to the GOJ, assesses for bleeding, assesses for abdominal distention, communicates with anaesthetic team

1 - Very poor = Does not push in adequately, does not utilize pressure with the cap to create traction, inadequate space between the cutting line and mucosa. Pushes in aggressively dissecting using pressure of the advancing scope

5 - Very Good = Pushes in the scope with controlled pressure. Uses the cap to create gentle traction at the advancing submucosal muscle interface

1 - Very poor = Unaware of the GOJ, continues dissection without being aware that the tunnel has crossed the GOJ, poor insight into the vasculature changes in the cardia. Dissects too deeply into the cardia or does not reach the GOJ

5 - Very Good = Anticipates the GOJ, maintaining awareness of its position, carefully dissects into the cardia while avoiding large vessels and sling fibers. Care taken to not extend the tunnel too deep into the gastric cardia

1 - Very poor = Does not use insufflation to improve access despite repeated prompting

5 - Very Good = Appropriate use of insufflation to improve visualization of the submucosa muscle interface. Uses constant insufflation in areas of difficulty to ensure the mucosal surface is kept away from the cutting plane

5. Identification of the LES

1 - Very poor = Unable to identify the LES. Poor understanding of the techniques used to identify its location.

5 - Very Good = Confidently, accurately and independently able to identify the LES and confirms its location using several means.

6. Myotomy

1 - Very poor = Cranial end at or too close to the mucosotomy impairing safety. Caudal end beyond 3cm into the cardia risking post POEM GERD.

5 - Very Good = Correctly and justifiably identifies myotomy site/length. Understands the problems associated with a long gastric myotomy. Cranial extent starts distal to the mucosotomy.

1 - Very poor = Unable to appreciate circular vs longitudinal muscle layers. Unable to perform selective myotomy. Poor understanding of the role of each approach.

5 - Very good = Able to accurately identify the longitudinal and circular muscles. Where needed, careful selective dissection of the circular muscle layer is performed. Understands the pros and cons of both approaches.

1 - Very poor = Does not use injection to improve safety.

5 - Very good = Utilizes injection into the adventitial space to prevent thermal injury to mediastinal structures when using the mucosal to adventitial approach.

1 - Very poor = Inappropriate choice of technique, use of adventitial to mucosal technique in small spaces, applies excessive traction on the muscle with the knife. Excessive uncontrolled use of thermal area, incising large areas of muscle at a time. Mentor take over due to safety.

5 - Very good = Insightful choice of approach based on the amount of space in the tunnel. Avoids excessive traction on the muscle with the knife. Slow, controlled, stepwise incisions made. Able to seamlessly switch between approaches. Maintains knife perpendicular to the circular muscle.

1 - Very poor = Risky use of thermal energy close to the mucosa. Insufficient use of insufflation compromising safety. Unsafe necessitating mentor take over.

5 - Very good = Good use of insufflation to keep the mucosal surface away from the muscle. When using the AM approach ensures a safe margin from the mucosal surface before applying thermal energy.

1 - Very poor = Fails to assess for completeness. Does not recognize remaining circular muscle.

5 - Very good = Meticulously inspects the myotomy to ensure complete disruption of the circular layer.

7. Haemostasis

1 - Very poor = Fails to identify large vessels cutting into them. Poor awareness of tunnel direction and which vessels can be avoided.

5 - Very good = Able to identify large vessels timeously and dissect around them. Able to estimate bleeding risk based on size of vessels. Appreciates the higher risk of bleeding in the cardia.

1 - Very poor = Unable to identify vessels and pretreat. Poor understanding of bleeding risk based on vessel size. Incorrect current used, vessels cut instead of coagulated. Incorrect.

5 - Very good = Appropriately chooses instrument (knife vs coagulation grasper) based on vessel size. Dissects around vessels and uses slow, controlled application of coagulation current to vessels prior to cutting. Precise use of the knife or coagulation grasper coagulation and ensures the entire vessel is grasped before coagulating. Applies coagulation long enough to ensure the vessel is adequately treated.

1 - Very poor = Does not routinely assess for minor bleeding after myotomy. Unable to use water as an aid.

5 - Very good = Routinely instills water into the tunnel noting discoloration and subsequently looks for bleeding source.

1 - Very poor = Does not asses for transected vessel or failure to identify transected vessels.

5 - Very good = Carefully assess the tunnel for transected vessels and appropriately treats prior to tunnel closure.

8. Tunnel closure

1 - Very poor = Fails to orientate the mucosotomy in the 6 or 12 o'clock position.

5 - Very good = Easily and independently maintains the mucosotomy in the 6 or 12 o'clock position for closure.

1 - Very poor = Unable to place the first clip in an appropriate position. Begins closure at incorrect position. Requires mentor take over.

5 - Very good = Independently places the first clip on normal mucosa at the caudal end of the mucosotomy facilitating zipper closure. Appreciates the effect of gravity on the shaft of the clips.

1 - Very poor = Unable to close mucosotomy. Inaccurate, uneven, incorrect position of clips. Deploys clips prior to confirming position. Risks injury to the mucosa. Persistent tissue inversion and unable to identify eversion. Unsafe closure warranting mentor take over.

5 - Very good = Even, accurate, independent placement of clips ensuring zipper type closure. Good communication with nursing staff. Ensures adequate position and tissue eversion prior to deploying clips.

1 - Very poor = Fails to recognize need for additional clips or poorly sited clips as a reason for inadequate closure. Attempts to achieve closure by adding multiple clips without resiting poorly placed clips. Unable to achieve closure despite mentor input.

5 - Very good = Recognizes need for additional clips. Able to identify poorly sited clips which hamper progression of closure. Safely removes clips and independently able to resite achieving tight closure.

1 - Very poor = Fails to assess completeness of closure risking leaks post procedure.

5 - Very good = Meticulously assess the entire mucosotomy for complete closure. Uses the cap to check areas between clips.

9. Complications

1 - Very poor = Unable to clear working field and locate bleeding point. Persistently bloody field, inaccurate blind use of coagulation. Necessitates mentor take over.

5 - Very good = Precisely, independently and quickly identifies bleeding points. Uses the water jet, cap and image enhancement to identify bleeding points. Utilizes the cap to tamponade vessels while changing instruments.

1 - Very poor = Applies thermal energy close to the mucosa risking injury.

5 - Very good = Recognizes the low bleeding risk of the devascularized mucosal surface. Lifts vessels away from the mucosal surface before applying thermal energy.

1 - Very poor = Incorrect modality selected for treating bleeding.

5 - Very good = Appropriately selects modality for treating bleeding based on severity and size. Uses closed knife for small vessels and coagulation grasper for larger vessels.

1 - Very poor = Unable to manage bleeding despite mentor input. Uncontrolled, blind use of coagulation. Current applied for too short a period resulting in further bleeding or too much current used risking thermal injury. Unaware of the need to protect the mucosal surface from thermal injury.

5 - Very good = Precise, controlled, slow, independent use of coagulation to stop bleeding. Precise use of the closed knife on the bleeding point. Entire vessel grasped with the coagulation grasper, vessel lifted away from mucosa prior to thermal ablation. Correct duration of coagulation is used to treat vessels and minimize injury to surrounding tissue.

1 - Very poor = Fails to identify mucosal injury, unaware how to recognize injury.

5 - Very good = Repeatedly checks mucosal integrity without prompting, looks for blanching and perforation. Recognizes loss in distention with insufflation.

1 - Very poor = Fails to identify mucosal perforations and poor insight into closure techniques. Enlarges perforations with excessive use of insufflation and uncontrolled scope movements.

5 - Very good = Quickly recognizes mucosal injury and able to justify technique for closure. Displays good knowledge into the limitations and benefits of closure techniques.

1 - Very poor = Unable to securely close perforations requiring mentor take over.

5 - Very good = Independently, safely and securely manages perforations. Meticulously inspects. Able to complete the procedure after closure.

1 - Very poor = Fails to recognize capnoperitoneum.

5 - Very good = Frequent communication with the anaesthetics team and examination of the patient for abdominal distention. Understands the cardiorespiratory consequences of gas related complications. Able to recognize capnoperitoneum timeously.

1 - Very poor = Does not recognize indications for needle decompression. Stops the procedure at an inappropriate point.

5 - Very good = Understands indications for needle decompression, leads the team effectively, stops the procedure when safe to do so to perform decompression.

1 - Very poor = Unable to safely perform needle decompression despite mentor input.

5 - Very good = Confirms presence of capnoperitoneum, identifies site of decompression, cleans overlying skin, adequate depth of insertion, ensures bubbling through a water-filled syringe, secures catheter in place, and monitors bubbling.

10. Global competencies

1 - Very poor = Jerky, inaccurate, large, non-purposeful movements. Unable to optimize planes and orientation to achieve objectives. Excessive force. Compromising safety and warranting mentor take over.

5 - Very good = Controlled, precise, fine, purposeful movements. Uses right hand rotation, manipulation of the wheels and rotation to achieve optimal position. Demonstrates excellent control of the knife and other accessories.

1 - Very poor = Repeatedly unable to identify planes and the cutting line despite mentor input, compromising safety.

5 - Very good = Good, independent appreciation of planes and cutting line at all stages of the procedure. Anticipates areas of difficulty and able to optimize technique to improve the plane and safely proceed.

1 - Very poor = Fails to perform any periodic checks.

5 - Very good = Frequently checks ESU settings before using thermal energy (after every change in settings), use of CO2 rather than air, mucosal integrity, abdominal distention and maintains good communication with the anaesthetics team.

1 - Very poor = Fails to achieve good orientation despite mentor input. Unaware of or ineffective use of withdrawal, advancement and rotation of the scope.

5 - Very good = Able to easily achieve orientation, tunnel easily advanced perpendicular to the circular muscle. In more difficult cases achieves a good orientation with repeated withdrawal, advancement and rotation of the scope.
